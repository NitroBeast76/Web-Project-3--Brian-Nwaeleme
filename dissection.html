Communities and Affiliations

Seven communities were involved in Treaty 1, all are part of the Anishinaabe (Ojibwe) and Swampy Cree (Muskegon Cree) ethnic groups:

  • Peguis First Nation
  • Roseau River Anishinaabe First Nation
  • Brokenhead Ojibway Nation
  • Sagkeeng First Nation
  • Long Plain First Nation
  • Sandy Bay Ojibway Nation
  • Swan Lake First Nation #2 & #3


Written Terms of Treaty 1

  1. Reserve Lands: 160 acres per family of five (proportional for smaller or larger families) were given to each band.
  2. Gratuities and Annuities: Each person received 3 dollars as a one time payment, and a yearly payment of $15 per family of five.
  3. Education: The Indigenous were promised education, and schools were built in the reserves.
  4. Prohibition on the sale or introduction of liquor on reserves
  5. Cession of Land: The Indigenous were in turn asked to cede or surrender a massive portion of land of present day south-east and south-central Manitoba

Outside Promises of Treaty 1

  1. Agricultural Tools: Agricultural animals like bulls, cows, etc and equipment like hoes, cutlasses etc were given to begin.
  2. Hunting and Fishing: The Indigenous were allowed to hunt and fish in the area, as long as it was not on the land that was ceded.
  3. Protection: The Indigenous were promised protection from the Canadian Government, and that they would not be disturbed by settlers.

Uniqueness ofTreaty 1

  1. It is the first Numbered Treaty, Post-Confederation. It stated teh formal treaty making, allocating and shaping Canada as a whole.
  2. The outside promises controversy also made oit very unique as it was one with many debates and arguments about the authenticity of oral statements.
  3. The amount of land given was smaller than what was demanded by the Indigenous leaders later, apart from Treaty 5
  4. The right to fish and hunt were not explicit
  5. It had cultural clashes as teh Canadian government meant total surrender while the Indigenous meant mutual sharing
  6. It greatly impacted later treaties #5

Implementation of Treaty 1

  • Kapyong Urban Reserve: This urban reserve in 2019 is an example of this treaty, as it is under this treaty that the Treaty 1 nations acquired and are developing the Kapyong Barracks into an urban reserve
  • Naawi-Oodena Urban Reserve: This urban reserve is similar to the Kapyong Urban Reserve as the Treaty 1 Nations are also massively developing them to become urban, metropolitan areas.
  • Economic and Urban Development: Treaty 1 nations are striving hard and increasing their opportunities for their future generations, through their commercial and economic activities, establishing businesses all around their reserves and building urban reserves like Kapyong and Naawi-Oodena
  • Organizations like Treaty One Development Corporation are responsible and implement the treaty by acquiring land to sign under the treaty as reserves #6 #7
